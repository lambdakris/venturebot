## Emergency Information

### Emergency Action Plan

In the event of a serious incident:

1. **Scene Safety:** Ensure scene is safe for rescuers. Move patient from immediate hazards if possible.
2. **First Aid:** Provide immediate care - stop bleeding, assess breathing, stabilize injuries.
3. **Determine Location:** Get precise coordinates using GPS/phone.
4. **Call for Help:**
   - Activate SOS on satellite messenger (primary method)
   - Call 911 if cell service available
5. **Provide Information:**
   - Exact location (coordinates)
   - Number in party, number injured
   - Nature of emergency and patient condition
   - Equipment available (rope, shelter, warm clothing)
6. **Stay Put:** Do not move unless instructed by rescue team. Keep patient warm.

## Template Notes

**Required Sections:** Summary, Participants, Transportation, Route, Conditions, Equipment, Emergency Information

**Conditional Sections (include when relevant):**
- Avalanche Forecast: Winter/spring snow routes
- Technical Sections: Routes with rappels, exposed climbing, etc.
- Bailout Options: Technical routes with commitment
- Technical Gear: Routes requiring specialized equipment
- Objective Hazards: Routes with significant hazards
- Sources: When you want to credit specific resources

**Formatting Guidelines:**
- Use tables for participant info, route segments, and emergency contacts
- Include links to all maps (CalTopo, Google Maps)
- Use bold for key information and headers
- Include specific times (not just "morning" but "6:30 AM")
- Be specific with numbers (elevation, distance, duration)
- Include coordinates for key waypoints
- Link all weather/forecast resources
